Meet Bearing

Your AI Fluency
Assessment Agent.

Bearing is an AI Fluency Assessment agent built specifically for product managers. It runs as a specialized agent within Claude.ai, guiding you through a structured conversation about your real work rather than asking you to rate yourself on a scale. Bearing is trained on the AI Fluency Framework for Product Managers and produces a scored, narrative report across two dimensions: Efficiency AI and Opportunity AI. Think of it less as a test and more as a calibration conversation with someone who knows the framework deeply.

What you receive

Two scores. One honest picture.

The assessment measures Efficiency AI and Opportunity AI independently. A PM can be highly proficient in one while still early in the other, and most are. Understanding both gives you a complete picture of where to focus.

Dimension 01

Efficiency AI

How well you use AI to do your existing work faster and better. The foundation of AI fluency and where most people start.

Foundational
Occasional, reactive use. Still discovering what's possible.
Applied
Consistent, intentional use across most PM responsibilities.
Advanced
Systematized workflows. Agents handle substantial work asynchronously.

Dimension 02

Opportunity AI

How well you use AI to do things that were not previously possible at all. The dimension that separates good AI fluency from transformative.

Foundational
Beginning to ask what AI makes possible. Instinct developing.
Applied
Regularly uses AI for things impossible without it. The ceiling has moved.
Advanced
Consistently expands what the team is capable of. Builds things that wouldn't otherwise exist.

About your report

The detailed report Bearing produces is private and intended for you alone. Before any data is used for team or organizational reporting, it is scrubbed of personally identifiable information. An anonymous scoring summary is used to support aggregated reporting that helps leaders understand where training and resources are needed across their team or organization.

If you'd like to see what your anonymous summary looks like, you can ask Bearing to share it at any point during or after the assessment.

Not a quiz.
A conversation.

Most self-assessments ask you to rate yourself on a scale. This one asks about real work and probes when your answers are vague. That's what makes the score meaningful.

Step 01

Answer questions about real work

Bearing will ask you about how you actually work, not how you think you would work in a hypothetical. Specific examples reveal more than self-ratings ever could.

Step 02

Bearing probes for specifics

If an answer is vague, Bearing will ask a follow-up. This isn't a gotcha; it's how the assessment stays honest. "I use AI a lot" is not evidence. A concrete example is.

Step 03

Receive your score and priorities

At the end you'll receive a score on both dimensions, a narrative grounded in what you said, and three concrete next steps ordered by impact for your seniority level.

Before you begin

What to expect

Format

Conversational, spoken or typed

Bearing leads the conversation across all six domains. You can type your responses or use your device's dictation mode. Voice answers tend to be richer and faster.

Time commitment

15–30 minutes

Specific, concrete answers move things along. Longer if the conversation goes deep in a particular area.

Scoring

Evidence-based, no self-rating

Bearing scores what you demonstrate, not how you describe yourself. A Foundational score is an accurate starting point, not a bad result.

Getting an accurate score

Be specific. Be honest.

Real examples from actual work produce accurate scores. Vague answers produce vague results. Bearing is looking for your real starting point, not a flattering one.

What you'll need

A Claude account (free tier works)

The assessment runs inside Claude.ai. If you don't have an account, you can create one for free at claude.ai.
