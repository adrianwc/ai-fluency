Efficiency AIAdvanced
Opportunity AIApplied
You move from data to decision faster than almost anyone at your level. You critically evaluate AI-generated conclusions, stress-test the reasoning, and arrive at conversations with a point of view already formed.
Why this matters at your level: At the Senior PM level, decision velocity is a multiplier. A PM who can synthesise quickly and push back on AI recommendations shapes how the whole team uses data, not just their own work.
What to do with this strength: Lead data reviews for the team. Your ability to synthesise quickly and interrogate AI outputs is something peers can learn from directly.
Vision Articulation & Product Storytelling
Efficiency AIAdvanced
Opportunity AIAdvanced
Your ability to construct and communicate a compelling product narrative is Advanced on both dimensions — AI has not only made this work faster, it has changed what is possible. You use AI to prepare for leadership conversations, structure business cases, and pressure-test your logic before walking into the room. On the Opportunity side, you have also begun designing experiences where the product narrative itself is adaptive, shaped by real-time signal rather than static positioning.
Why this matters at your level: Persuasive communication becomes a competitive advantage as scope grows. Senior PMs who can build airtight narratives at speed spend less time in alignment loops and more time driving decisions — and those who use AI to shape the product story, not just present it, operate at a different level entirely.
What to do with this strength: Apply this to your next major investment case. Build a multi-horizon narrative with AI, use it to anticipate the objections you will face in the room, and document the process so others on the team can replicate it.
Cross-System & Stakeholder Alignment at Speed
Efficiency AIAdvanced
Opportunity AIApplied
You drive alignment across functions and stakeholders with a speed and precision that is above expectation for your level. You use AI to prepare for difficult conversations, anticipate objections, coordinate across competing priorities, and arrive in rooms already having stress-tested the positions you will face.
Why this matters at your level: Senior PMs who align stakeholders quickly without repeated escalation multiply their own scope. This is often the difference between a roadmap that moves and one that stalls in review cycles — and at Advanced on the Efficiency side, you are clearly on the right side of that line.
What to do with this strength: Use this skill to build alignment infrastructure, not just alignment moments. Design shared dashboards, pre-read templates, and communication rhythms so the team runs with less friction even when you are not in the room.

Section 04
Your Personalized Development Roadmap

The roadmap below is sequenced by leverage and effort. Start with Tier 1 — these are moves you can make within your current work without taking on new projects. Tier 2 requires more intentionality but will have the largest impact on your profile over the next quarter. Tier 3 is the horizon you are building toward.

A note on these recommendations. This roadmap is built from your assessment profile and is intended as a starting point for your own judgment — not a directive. Whether and how to pursue each item will depend on your organization's culture, your manager's priorities, and the tools and resources available to you. Items tagged May require… have prerequisites that may be outside your direct control to create on your own, and are best approached as conversations with your manager or team rather than actions you take unilaterally.
Tier 1 — Quick Wins
Next 30 days
  • Build your first reusable prompt workflowSelf-directedPick one recurring task (a weekly data review, competitive scan, or stakeholder update) and write a prompt for it this week. Save it somewhere you will actually use it and run it three times before revising.
  • AI-first planning prepSelf-directedBefore your next planning session, run your full qualitative feedback backlog through AI synthesis before you look at any of it manually. Note where the AI-generated themes surprised you versus confirmed what you already believed.
  • Set up a competitive monitoring workflowSelf-directedWrite a prompt that monitors one specific competitor's product releases and summarises changes on a weekly cadence. Treat this as a workflow you run consistently, not a one-off query.
Tier 2 — Priority Development
Next 90 days
  • Design your first async agent workflowSelf-directedIdentify one research or analysis task that currently requires your active attention and redesign it as a background workflow. The target is a usable output produced without you monitoring it step by step.
  • Own the next experiment designMay require manager alignmentDesign the next experiment your team runs rather than delegating it. Use AI to check your sample sizing, identify confounds, and validate the measurement approach. Share it with a data partner for a quick sanity check before kicking it off.
  • Practice PM–engineer pairingMay require team buy-inIn your next sprint, work through one feature in dialogue with an engineer rather than writing a spec first. Let the conversation be the requirements artifact and debrief afterward on what worked.
  • Translate competitive intelligence to roadmap decisionsSelf-directedTake your next competitive intelligence summary and write a direct translation where each observation resolves to a specific build, defer, or monitor decision on your roadmap.
Tier 3 — Building Toward
6+ months
  • Build an autonomous competitive research loopSelf-directedDesign an agent that pulls competitive signals, synthesises them against your current roadmap priorities, and flags where those priorities may need to shift. This is infrastructure-level thinking applied to your growth domain.
  • Lead metric and instrumentation designMay require manager alignmentTake ownership of instrumentation design for your next major feature. Define leading and lagging indicators, specify logging requirements, and define what measurable good looks like for AI-generated outputs.
  • Redesign team discovery with AI as a participantMay require organizational supportRebuild your team's discovery process so AI is a first-class input, with continuous behavioral signal monitoring, AI-synthesised interview analysis, and always-on competitive tracking feeding directly into planning.

Your Next 5 Actions
